Five Pillars of Job Architecture

1

Job Family Classification

Categorize roles into functional groupings (e.g., Marketing, Engineering, HR). This creates the vertical "buckets" that allow for distinct market benchmarking.

Standardization Clarity
2

Leveling Matrix (The Leveling Framework)

Define the hierarchy across Individual Contributors, Managers, and Executives. We apply factors like Complexity, Scope, and Autonomy to differentiate between a Senior and a Lead.

3

Job Title Standardization

Harmonize internal titles to market standards. Move away from "Creative Ninjas" toward "Senior Graphic Designer" to ensure external equity and recruitment ease.

4

Market Mapping & Evaluation

The science of rewards. Link your internal levels to external salary surveys. This ensures your pay ranges are competitive while maintaining internal consistency.

5

Career Path Mapping

Finally, we visualize the path. Show employees how they can move from IC level 3 to Management or into a different Job Family entirely. This drives retention.

Expert Insights for Success

Designing the framework is only half the battle. Change management and stakeholder buy-in are what make a job architecture stick.

  • Focus on the Role, Not the Person: Architecture describes what the business needs, not the current incumbent's skills.
  • Keep it Simple: Aim for 8-10 global levels. Complexity is the enemy of adoption.
  • Future Proof: Design for the company you want to be in 3 years, not just where you are today.
